#1

LUSH Games and
Entertainment

Across the internet, ad games are widely used for marketing products and brands. Improved marketing and brand awareness are really good outcomes from this technique. The interaction, the competition and sense of reward that games generate are good ways of boosting engagement.

Some big names in the market use this technique quite often and successfully. McDonald's, Nike, Unilever and others use games to leverage a campaign and improve the engagement of the customer.


Successful examples of ad games

Magnum - Pleasure Hunt



This games was part of the a marketing campaign and consisted in allowing the player to travel around the world in the game to capture chocolate and other tokens related to the their main product.

Heineken - Bottle Slider Game

In this game, the player uses both the computer and the smartphone to slide bottles across a virtual track. The player pulls the bottles using the smartphone. The result is reflected on the computer screen.


What about LUSH Games ?

LUSH products have all the elements a Game Designer love: they have shapes, colours and a story. When we think about Intergalactic, for examples, there are multiple stories you can produce and tell about it - there's inifinity, exploration, adventure, stars, dust and all the beauty of the unknown.

Angry birds is a successful example of a game that produced a business, where toys are sold using the Angry Birds brand. LUSH could use the same model, but on the other way around: we already have the toys, why don't we have the games to popularize our "toys" even more ? Here's a good opportunity to make people want the products just because they have fun with them on a game.

Games is a good strategy because of one thing: It's fun to play!

2.1 Product Collection - My Products

LUSH Connect is one incredible tool to entertain Lushies. I feel LUSH could add a few things to keep the users in the app for longer periods. One idea is to allow the user to capture products they purchase or see in the website and keep them in their passports, just like they do when they find a badge at an event.

The difference in this approach is the way the products would be kept in the app. They would be stored as 3D Objects and LUSH Connect would be able to show them and allow the user to rotate and pinch the product on the screen.

The idea is to engage the user to capture products and see how they look like in 3D - trigger the sense of possession. Even a ranking of Lush collectors can be created to improve the engagement of who has more products in their collections.

A new icon would be added to Lush Connect's main screen. Along with the options to collect badges at events like Summit and Creative Showcase, this icon would open a scrollable catalogue of 3D products you might have collected

Advantages

  • Users stay longer in the app
  • Users can see and even show others how a product looks like
  • Healthy competition between users to see who has more products in their collection


2.2 Product Collection - Capture

The capture of the product can be done using Lush Lens. With Augmented Reality and 3D technology Lush Lens can improve the ability of the user to see products and have a better experience.

One complaint I have, which I share with other customers, is the lack of notion of the dimension of the product when surfing Lush website. If the website had a 3D version of the product where the user could explore the product by rotating and pinching, it would be a great experience. However, 3D can be difficult to embed on a website and still wouldn't give the full dimension of a product. That's where Augmented Reality steps in.
